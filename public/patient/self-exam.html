Guided Oral Self-Examination

Perform this mirror-assisted check once a month. Catching red/white mucosal patches, ulcers, or swelling early plays a major role in oral cancer prevention.

Step 1 of 5

Mirror-Assisted Setup & Prep

Prepare your examination area. Find a bright, clear mirror in a well-lit room. You can also use a smartphone flashlight to illuminate the inside of your mouth.

Initial Checklist

  • Wash hands thoroughly with soap and warm water.
  • Remove any dental plates, retainers, or dentures.
  • Adjust your light source to shine directly into the mouth.
Position yourself in front of a well-lit mirror with hands washed and dentures removed.
Step 2 of 5

Retract Your Lips & Check Gums

Gently pull your upper lip upward and your lower lip downward. Visually inspect the inner lip lining and gums. Run your fingers over the tissue to feel for lumps or texture changes.

What to look for

  • Velvety red spots or raw surface textures on gums.
  • Thickened white plaques that don't wash off.
  • Sores on the inner lip that fail to heal.

Step 2 Checkup findings

Gently retract upper and lower lips to visually check the gum lines and inner mucosa.
Step 3 of 5

Examine Lateral Tongue Borders

Stick your tongue straight out. Wrap a clean tissue or gauze around the tip to hold it gently. Pull the tongue to the left and then to the right, looking closely at the lateral borders.

High-Risk Zones

  • The sides of the tongue are the most common site for oral lesions.
  • Look for red, velvety ulcers or firm nodules.
  • Check if side borders feel hardened (indurated) to touch.

Step 3 Checkup findings

Inspect both lateral margins of the tongue, as the sides are high-risk zones.
Step 4 of 5

Inspect Buccal Mucosa & Palate

Use the handle of a clean spoon or your fingers to pull your cheeks outward. Inspect the inner cheek linings. Tilt your head back to inspect the hard and soft palate (roof of the mouth).

Inspection Areas

  • Examine cheek linings on both sides for spots or bite lines.
  • Inspect the palate for color changes, rough spots, or lumps.
  • Feel the tissues to check for uniform texture.

Step 4 Checkup findings

Tilt your head back to inspect the palate (roof) and pull the cheeks out to check inner linings.
Step 5 of 5

Examine Floor of Mouth & Throat

Raise the tip of your tongue to the roof of your mouth. Inspect the floor of the mouth beneath the tongue. Swallow slowly to test for any soreness, throat pain, or difficulty opening your jaw.

Screening parameters

  • Unexplained swelling or hard nodes underneath the tongue.
  • Sores or ulcerations on the sublingual salivary areas.
  • Noticeable pain or tightness when opening the jaw wide.

Step 5 Checkup findings

Raise your tongue tip to check the sublingual floor area and glands beneath.
