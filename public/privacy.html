Patient Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 19, 2026 | RTooth Platform Compliance

1. Introduction and Scope

RTooth ("we," "us," or "our") operates the RTooth clinical management and patient monitoring software. We are committed to protecting the privacy, security, and integrity of Protected Health Information (PHI) and Personal Data collected from patients, clinics, and oncologists.

This Privacy Policy outlines how we collect, use, store, share, and protect your clinical records, symptom logs, and diagnostic photograph uploads in accordance with applicable laws, including the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

2. Information We Collect

In order to provide secure care coordination services, we collect several categories of information:

  • Demographic Information: Patient name, email address, phone number, physical address, and date of birth.
  • Clinical and Health Information: Assigned oncologist details, diagnostic lesion photographs, mouth opening measurements, medical history, active medications, and patient self-reported symptom logs.
  • Technical Data: IP addresses, browser info, session metadata, and system access logs for administrative security audits.

3. HIPAA Compliance & Patient Rights

Any health data uploaded to RTooth is managed in strict alignment with HIPAA regulations. Patients have the following privacy rights regarding their health information:

  • Access and Copying: You have the right to review and retrieve your complete clinical history, prescriptions, and uploaded photographs via your patient portal dashboard.
  • Access Auditing: The system logs all database access logs. IT-Admins track which specialists have retrieved your records to ensure RLS (Row Level Security) policies are properly restricting queries.
  • Data Restrictions: You can request that your clinic restricts specific sharing permissions, though this may limit your care coordinator's diagnostic ability.

4. How We Use Your Information

We use the collected information solely to facilitate early oral dysplasia detection and clinical care coordination:

  • To display and track diagnostic timelines for your attending oncologist.
  • To trigger clinical warning feeds if self-reported symptom logs cross risk thresholds.
  • To coordinate appointment schedules and send follow-up compliance reminders.
  • To log administrative logs for HIPAA audit purposes. We never sell, trade, or distribute your private health data to marketing networks.

5. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, RLS security controls, or RTooth's HIPAA compliance protocols, please contact our administrative coordinator at compliance@rtooth.com.
